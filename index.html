About Me

Hi! I am Shawon. I am currently a PhD Candidate at the University of Washington Information School, Seattle. My advisor is Dr. Chirag Shah and I am a member of InfoSeeking Lab and Data Lab.

My research focuses on understanding how people seek, interpret and use information and their information needs and tasks to assist them with task completion.

My research interests intersect interactive information retrieval, human-computer interaction, and machine learning. Recently, my research revolves around building task-based search personalization models and intelligent systems using mixed-method design approaches and various human-centered AI techniques to support people in accomplishing their tasks. I aim to use my research to increase people's capability to access information and improve information systems to alleviate barriers people face while seeking information.

I was previously in the Ph.D. Program in Communication, Information and Media at the School of Communication and Information (SC&I), Rutgers, The State University of New Jersey, New Brunswick. Prior to joining PhD, I earned my Master of Information in Data Science from the department of Library and Information Science at the SC&I, Rutgers University. Before that, I completed my Masters and Bachelors in English Literature and Language in India.

In my free time, I love cooking, painting and traveling to new places. I also take great pleasure in stamp collecting.

For more information, please reach out to me! Or, have a look at my research projects, publications and activities, or CV.
