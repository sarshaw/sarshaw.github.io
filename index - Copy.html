About Me

I am a fourth year PhD student at the Information School, University of Washington, Seattle. My advisor is Dr. Chirag Shah and I am a member of InfoSeeking Lab and Data Lab at iSchool.

My research focuses on the intersection of Interactive Information Retrieval, Human-Computer Interaction, and Human-centered AI. I seek to apply the knowledge learned from people interacting with information in search recommendation, user education and intelligent nudging. Through my research, I try to understand how people seek, use and interpret information in both virtual and real-life environment based on their needs and intentions, and other situational contexts using various qualitative and quantitaive research approaches. I aim to use my research findings to increase people's awareness and capability of searching information, and help design and improve information systems and services to alleviate barriers people face while seeking for information.
